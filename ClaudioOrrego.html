Claudio Orrego

Political Content Strategy & Live Streaming

In 2021, during the height of the pandemic, I was brought on as a content strategist and video producer for Claudio Orrego’s gubernatorial campaign in Santiago, Chile. My role spanned daily video production, real-time event coverage, and the execution of a livestream strategy that helped him secure the election.

Every day, I produced, edited, and published short-form content documenting Claudio’s movements — from local visits to key policy messages. I traveled alongside the team to film and deliver fresh content within hours. Our goal: maintain visibility and relatability across platforms without delay.


In addition to filming and editing daily content, I developed a full multi-platform content calendar to organize the campaign’s digital communication across Instagram, Facebook, and Twitter. This ensured a steady stream of high-quality posts tied to Claudio’s field visits, weekly themes, and key policy moments. I also created short-form video series like “Una Mañana con Claudio”, which followed his day-to-day in a cinematic, accessible format, and “Lo Que Viene”, a weekly recap and forecast used to maintain narrative continuity with voters.

To expand reach, I identified and activated a younger voter segment by experimenting with meme formats. By inserting humor and irony into political messaging, I was able to grow Instagram engagement by over 160%, especially among 18–30 year olds in urban districts. These meme posts generated up to 4x the interaction rate of traditional policy content, making them essential for final-stage visibility and relatability.

On the visual side, I animated the campaign’s official logo and exported it in .mov format with alpha transparency. This allowed the internal editing team to plug and play the logo across reels, livestreams, and video edits — reducing turnaround time and keeping all assets visually unified. I also developed video templates and motion graphics systems that were later reused by five different editors across the campaign’s media operations.One of my most impactful contributions was the design and execution of the “Cabildos” livestream system. I branded and streamed 52 community-led digital town halls using OBS and Zoom, one for each comuna of Santiago. These town halls averaged over 600 live viewers and maintained an average watch time of 7+ minutes — unusually high for political streaming. The format not only humanized the campaign but also allowed Claudio to have real-time dialogue with constituents across the city, reinforcing transparency and trust.

These strategies played a measurable role in the campaign’s success. Claudio Orrego won the election, with record turnout in key districts that overlapped directly with our livestream and youth outreach efforts. The combination of rapid content production, meme-based virality, and consistent livestreaming created a digital backbone that kept the campaign present and personal in the middle of a pandemic.

Contributions

  • Developed a multi-platform content calendar covering daily posts for Instagram, Facebook, and Twitter, structured around field visits, policy themes, and key public sentiment windows
  • Identified and activated a youth voter segment by creating and deploying viral meme content, tapping into humor to drive shareability and boost relatability
  • Designed and animated the campaign’s official motion logo, exported in .mov format for plug-and-play use by internal editors — accelerating content production across all video formats
  • Created modular templates for stories, reels, and short-form vertical video used by other team members, improving content speed and brand consistency
  • Conceptualized, branded, and deployed “Cabildos”: livestreamed digital town halls — one for each of Santiago’s 52 comunas — streamed via OBS and Zoom with real-time interactions across all social media platforms.
  • Produced short-form documentary-style edits and edited full motion graphics for Claudio Orrego's Jingles.

Results

  • Boosted youth engagement by +160% on Instagram through meme-based reach and comments
  • Weekly content output scaled from 3 to 10 assets per week with templated workflows and .mov logo exports
  • “Cabildos” town halls streamed across 52 districts with average watch time of 7+ minutes — a rare feat in political streaming
  • Campaign-wide visual assets unified under a motion design system used by 5+ internal editors
  • Meme engagement exceeded traditional policy posts by 4x interaction rate during final 3 weeks
  • Claudio Orrego won the governorship
